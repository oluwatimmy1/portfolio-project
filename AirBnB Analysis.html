HYPOTESIS FORMULATION/TESTING

HYPOTHESIS 1: NEIGHBOURHOOD GROUP VERSUS PRICE

  • NULL HYPOTHESIS (0): The average price across the neighbourhood groups is the same.
  • ALTERNATIVE HYPOTHESIS (1): The average price across the neighbourhood groups is different.

HYPOTHESIS 2: MINIMUM NIGHTS VS PRICE

  • NULL HYPOTHESIS (H0): There is no linear relationship between the number of minimum nights spent and the price.
  • ALTERNATIVE HYPOTHESIS (H1): There is a linear relationship between the number of minimum nights spent and the price.

T-Test

  • This is a stat technnique used to compare the means of two groups to determine if they are significantly

different from each other. Exammples of this include - Independent T-Test: This compares the means of two independent groups. - Paired T-Test: This compares means from the same group at different times. - One-Sample T-Test: This compares the mean of a single group against a known value or proposition mean.

  • The T-Test can be used when we have one numerical and one categorical variable.

Hypothesis Formulation:

  • H0: The means of the two groups are equal.
  • H1: The means of the two groups are not equal.
